Chelsea School

STEAM Program

Summer 2015

In Brief

Through hands-on computing, Chelsea School's STEAM Summer Program provides day-long, hands-on instruction in science, technology, engineering, mathematics, & the arts for students in 4th through 12th grades.

From July 13th through the 24th, 2015 (9:00 AM - 4:30 PM), our weekdays begin with studying relationships between the culture, the arts, & innovative technologies.

In the afternoon, participants will learn fundamental programming concepts by programming tools with an innovative, visual programming language from MIT.

Following that joint session, participants may choose between Game Design & Development or Information Security & Forensics.

Goals

  • Promote literacy
  • Practice project management strategies
  • Encourage professional collaboration and consultation
  • Explore post-secondary programs, careers, and professional certification options
  • Discover programmatic solutions to common problems
  • Promote digital literacy
  • Practice mind-mapping and other discovery techniques
  • Identify sound and valid logic
  • Promote creative problem solving and critical thinking
  • Provoke innovation and invention

Features

Cybersecurity & Forensics

Attendees will learn & apply basic concepts of programming, computer forensics, cryptography, steganography, system vulnerability assessment, & project management from a series of gaming & simulation activities & hands-on exercises.

Game Development

Participants will have hands-on experience building a game in the Unity 4 software. Using 3D models & code produced in C# & Javascript, they will assemble the terrain, objects & other elements of the game, capture input from users & script game physics & AI behavior.

Programming Concepts

This game-based introduction to the logic underlying software engineering focuses on coding using visual & text-based languages in preparation for either our Game Design or Cybersecurity sessions.

State of the Art Facilities

Our new, state-of-the-art facility features interactive whiteboards; gigabit ethernet; & over 100 student workstations equipped for equitable access to appropriate assistive technology

Research-Based Instruction

Grounded in Chelsea School philosophy, participants will receive individualized instruction; reading across the curriculum; & post-secondary exploration & preparation from highly experienced instructors & technologists.

Arts & Technology

Participants will explore the role of arts & creativity in technology — including the ways in which literature, visual arts, & music influence — & are influenced by — science & technology. Participants will engage in hands-on projects including writing workshops, crafts & more.
